My Bottom Line

The Verdict

For most people in a home gym situation, Speediance wins on value. The $4,995 one-time price (with optional $39/mo membership) pays for itself compared to Tonal after about 2 years — and compared to a traditional gym after about 3-4 years.

With no wall installation required and 220 lbs of cable resistance, Speediance is the smarter buy for most home gym setups. The AI adaptation is genuinely impressive — it adjusts resistance mid-set in ways Tonal simply can't.

However, traditional gyms still make sense for some: If you want unlimited resistance (going heavy on squats/deadlifts), thrive in a social atmosphere, or want access to classes and amenities without buying equipment, a traditional gym at ~$150/mo is still a solid choice.

Tonal's content library and coaching quality are exceptional, and I had years of great workouts on it. But the required subscription and price hikes made it a harder sell. After 9M+ lbs, I'm happy with my decision to switch to Speediance.
